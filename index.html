Poem No. 1

Upon this roof, where Ivy climbs to claim its throne so grand,
The panel does sit in silent judgment of our sunny land—
Thirty days it counts with pride since the summer's fierce blaze began,
And here we stand amidst July’s second week; time is but a van.
‍
A dozen hours hath danced upon this stage today bright and clear,
Yet in its wake linger 120 sunny moments that all draw near—
The sum of which our panel has kept, while I muse on what's lost,
As energy giants hold a tight grip where freedom’s price is tossed.

Twelve hundred kWh should sparkle bright like stars in skies above;
Yet here they flicker dim within the grasp of monopoly—so cruel!
Imagine homes with warmth, and hearths aglow, so full of cheer,
But instead we pay for power that's squandered by those who hold it dear.

- the end
